As for those who say that what does not have a shadow is permissible, they are refuted by the general evidences and by specific evidences such as the ḥadīth of the cushion (namraqah).

Al-Bukhārī (5181) and Muslim (2107) narrated from ʿĀʾishah رضي الله عنها, the wife of the Prophet ﷺ, that she said: She informed him that she bought a cushion containing images. When the Messenger of Allah ﷺ saw it, he stood at the door and did not enter. I recognized displeasure on his face, so I said: “O Messenger of Allah, I repent to Allah and to His Messenger. What sin have I committed?” He said: “What is this cushion?” She said: “I bought it for you so that you may sit on it and recline upon it.” The Messenger of Allah ﷺ said:

**“Indeed, the makers of these images will be punished on the Day of Resurrection, and it will be said to them: ‘Give life to what you have created.’”**

And he said:

**“Indeed, the house in which there are images, the angels do not enter it.”**

Al-Nawawī said in Sharḥ Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim:

*“Some of the Salaf said: Prohibition applies only to what has a shadow, and there is no harm in images that have no shadow. This is a false madhhab, for the curtain in which the Prophet ﷺ denounced the image is unquestionably blameworthy, and its image had no shadow — along with the rest of the unrestricted aḥādīth concerning every image.”

*

I say:

Imām Aḥmad also held the view of prohibition. Ibn Qudāmah said in al-Mughnī (7/285):

It was said to Abū ʿAbdillāh: “A man rents a house in which there are images — do you think he should scrape them off?” He said: “Yes.”

Al-Marwadhī said: I said to Abū ʿAbdillāh: “I entered a bathhouse and saw an image — do you think I should remove the head?” He said: “Yes.”

This indicates that it was an image without a shadow.

And Allah knows best.

**— By Shaykha Umm ʿAbdillāh al-Wādiʿiyyah, may Allah preserve her
