In a recent class, a sister posed a question to Shaykha Umm ʿAbdillah Al-Brīṭāniyyah regarding note-taking.

Our Shaykha حفظها الله responded and said we should strive and try to make notes. She gives us time so we are able to write down notes; either by pausing or repeating things, or slowing down when translating etc.

She mentioned that, when she was studying in Dammāj, some teachers would give you some time to write down your notes as their pace was slower. However, in Sheikh Yaḥyā’s durūs, “he would **not** give you time” meaning he spoke very fast in his lessons making it difficult to jot down everything he says.

Therefore, Shaykha told us that, sometimes she would have blanks and would need to ask another sister for her notes in order to fill in the gaps. She would specifically go to a sister who she knows has memorised the ḥadīth and she is a sister who takes good notes.

However, we should not rely on other people’s notes as they could have mistakes and they may not get everything down.

She also encouraged us to write by hand because it helps us get used to writing in Arabic (that is when our lessons are in Arabic).

🖊️Umm Muṣʿab Al-Ghāniyyah Al-Almāniyyah
