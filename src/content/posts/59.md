Shaykha Umm ʿAbdillāh Al-wādiʿiyyah —حفظها الله— says:

**From the advice of some of my virtuous sisters to me:**

One of my sisters — may Allah preserve her — when she visited us from the Emirates, about five years before the death of my father, Shaykh Muqbil bin Hadi Al-Wadi'i, may Allah have mercy on him, she visited Dar Al-Hadith in Dammaj. We sat with her, and she mentioned a story: that Sa’eed ibn Al-Musayyib, when he wanted to leave his wife’s place to seek ḥadīth, she asked him: “Where are you going?” He said: “To Abu Hurayrah.” She said: “Sit down, for Abu Hurayrah's knowledge is with me.”

Sa’eed ibn Al-Musayyib was married to Abu Hurayrah’s daughter, may Allah be pleased with him. So she advised me: “Be like this — have your father’s knowledge with you!”

Some advice is very precious and valuable. Whoever does not benefit from it or neglects it will surely regret it and remember it with a broken heart full of regret and sorrow.

فلقدْ نصحتُكَ إنْ قبلتَ نصيحتي... فالنُّصحُ أغلى ما يُباعُ ويُوهَبُ

So I have advised you, if you accept my advice — For sincere advice is the most precious thing one can give or receive.

May Allah benefit us with such valuable advice.
