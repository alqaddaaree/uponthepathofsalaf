**Seeking Refuge with Allah from Tribulations (Fitnah)**

On the authority of Zayd ibn Thābit رضي الله عنه from the Prophet ﷺ — in the end of the ḥadīth he said:

> “Seek refuge with Allah from tribulations, those that are apparent and those that are hidden.”

They said: We seek refuge with Allah from tribulations, those that are apparent and those that are hidden.

He said: “Seek refuge with Allah from the tribulation of the Dajjāl.”

They said: We seek refuge with Allah from the tribulation of the Dajjāl.

رواه مسلم (2867)

this ḥadīth is the command to seek refuge from tribulations, both apparent and hidden.

**“What is apparent and what is hidden”** means: what is done openly and what is concealed. It is also said: what occurs outwardly upon a person, and what exists in the heart such as shirk (polytheism), showing off (riyā’), envy, and other blameworthy inner thoughts.

(Mirqāt al-Mafātīḥ 1/209)

This supplication is among the greatest of duʿā’ for seeking refuge from tribulations and their causes.

It includes asking for steadfastness, and it is general—covering all forms of tribulations.

Tribulations are many, and the later the time, the more they increase—becoming numerous, like pieces of a dark night, surging like the waves of the sea.

Among these tribulations are:

**• The tribulation of killing and fighting**

Al-Bukhārī (447) narrates from Abū Saʿīd al-Khudrī رضي الله عنه:

> We used to carry bricks one by one, while ʿAmmār carried two at a time. The Prophet ﷺ saw him, brushed the dust off him and said:

“Woe to ʿAmmār! He will be killed by the transgressing group. He calls them to Paradise, and they call him to the Fire.”

Abū Saʿīd said: ʿAmmār used to say: “I seek refuge with Allah from tribulations.”

**• The tribulation of the world and its desires

• The tribulation of illnesses and accidents

• The tribulation of losing loved ones and things dear to the soul

• The tribulation of children

• The tribulation of poverty and wealth

• The tribulation of people with one another**

Allah سبحانه وتعالى says:

*(And We have made some of you [people] as trial for others - will you have patience?)*

The tribulation of people with one another is many: A father may be tested through his child;

a man through his wife;

a woman through her husband;

one may be tested through a neighbor;

or through a companion whose bad manners affect him—as it is said: a companion drags you with him.

Or he may betray him despite good assumptions.

A ruler may also be tested, as his subjects overpower him, show him no respect, and do not recognize his rights over them.

**• Division and اختلاف (disagreement)**

This is among the greatest types of tribulations.

No one rejoices in tribulations except one in whom there is no khayr.

Rushing toward division and stirring it up indicates corruption of the inner self and absolute ignorance.

So beware of that, hold firmly to your religion, and be keen on uniting the word—this is from taqwā and from our aqidah.

Do not compromise your aqidah, whether you realize it or not.

Imām al-Ṭaḥāwī رحمه الله said in al-ʿAqīdah al-Ṭaḥāwiyyah:

> “We see unity as truth and correctness, and division as deviation and punishment.”

Abū Dāwūd (4263) narrates from al-Miqdād ibn al-Aswad (رضي الله عنه) who said:

> By Allah, I heard the Messenger of Allah ﷺ say:

“Indeed, the truly happy one is the one who is spared tribulations. Indeed, the truly happy one is the one who is spared tribulations. Indeed, the truly happy one is the one who is spared tribulations. And whoever is tested and remains patient—how excellent he is!”

O Allah, we seek refuge in You from tribulations.

O Allah, keep us firm upon Your religion until we meet You.

O Allah, we seek refuge in You from everything Your Prophet Muḥammad ﷺ sought refuge from.

**-Shaykha Umm 'Abdillāh Al-Wādiʿiyyah**
