**If you hope for emancipation (ʿitq) —from the Fire—, then buy yourself from Allāh:**

*{إِنَّ اللَّهَ اشْتَرَى مِنَ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ أَنْفُسَهُمْ وَأَمْوَالَهُمْ بِأَنَّ لَهُمُ الْجَنَّةَ} [التوبة: ١١١]*

*'Verily, Allāh has purchased of the believers their lives and their properties for (the price) that theirs shall be the Paradise.' [at-Tawbah: 111]*

Whoever holds their soul in high esteem will find any price easy to pay to ransom it from the Fire.

**Whoever knows what they are seeking will find easy whatever they must sacrifice.** Woe to you! We are well pleased with you if you ransom your soul through simple regret, and we are content with repentance and sorrow as its price.

In this blessed season, the price has become incredibly low; as the Prophet ﷺ said: “Whoever controls their hearing, their sight, and their tongue on it will be forgiven.”¹ Stretch out the hand of apology to Him, stand at His door in humility and brokenness, and submit the story of your regret written upon the page of your cheek with the ink of flowing tears, and say:

*"رَبَّنَا ظَلَمْنَا أَنفُسَنَا وَإِن لَّمْ تَغْفِرْ لَنَا وَتَرْحَمْنَا لَنَكُونَنَّ مِنَ الْخَاسِرِينَ"

'Our Lord, we have wronged ourselves, and if You do not forgive us and have mercy upon us, we will surely be among the losers.'*

> - Laṭāʾif al-Maʿārif p. 285

Tr: Umm ʿAbdirraḥmān as-Sūdāniyyah

*_*

1) The ḥadīth is graded ḍaʿīf (weak) by al-Albānī رحمه الله in as-Silsilah aḍ-Ḍaʿīfa no. 5960
