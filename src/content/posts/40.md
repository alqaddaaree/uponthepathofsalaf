**From the signs of the people of innovation:** **Rebellion against the rulers.**

*by: Shaykhah Umm ʿAbdillāh al-Wādiʿiyyah —حفظها الله—*:

Al-Dārimī narrated in his Sunan (no. 100) and al-Ājurrī in ash-Sharīʿah (no. 135) from Abū Qilābah, who said:

> “No man introduces an innovation except that he deems the sword lawful.”

﻿

This report is authentic—meaning rebellion against oppressive rulers.

This is among the consequences and evils of innovations. **No one rebels against the rulers except one who is upon innovation.** As for the one who is upon the Sunnah, he is the furthest of people from rebelling against the rulers.

Everyone who rebels against an unjust ruler has within him a Khariji, Muʿtazili inclination—whether from the Shīʿah, the Ikhwān al-Muslimīn, or others. This method is among the most prominent reprehensible traits of the people of innovation: rebelling against oppressive rulers and inciting the people to do so. This is contrary to the Sharīʿah and contrary to the sound aqīdah.

Imām al-Ṭaḥāwī—رحمه الله—said in al-ʿAqīdah aṭ-Ṭaḥāwiyyah:

> “We do not hold the view of rebelling against our leaders and those in authority over us, even if they are unjust.

We do not supplicate against them, nor do we withdraw our hand from obedience to them.

We consider obedience to them to be obedience to Allāh, the Mighty and Majestic, an obligation, so long as they do not command disobedience.

And we supplicate for them for rectitude and well-being.”

The Prophet ﷺ commanded listening and obedience to the ruler, warned against rebelling against him, commanded patience over his injustice, and commanded that the neck of the one who rebels against the ruler be struck.

Al-Bukhārī (no. 3793) narrated from Anas ibn Mālik رضي الله عنه that the Prophet ﷺ said to the Anṣār:

> “You will see after me selfishness (i.e., leaders giving preference to themselves) and matters which you will disapprove of. so be patient until you meet me, for your appointment is at the Ḥawḍ (the basin).”

Imām Muslim narrated in his Ṣaḥīḥ (no. 1846) from Wāʾil ibn Ḥujr al-Ḥaḍramī رضي الله عنه that Salamah ibn Yazīd al-Juʿfī asked the Messenger of Allāh ﷺ, saying:

*"O Messenger of Allāh, what do you think if rulers rise over us who demand their rights from us but withhold our rights—what do you command us?”*

He turned away from him. Then he asked him again, and he turned away. Then he asked him a second or third time, so al-Ashʿath ibn Qays pulled him and said:

> “Listen and obey, for upon them is what they are burdened with, and upon you is what you are burdened with.”

The people of innovation are a source of misfortune for the servants and the lands—in their calling, their teaching, and their revolutions—by distancing people from holding firmly to their religion. And the Messenger of Allāh ﷺ spoke truth when he said:

> “Indeed, the thing I fear most for my Ummah is the misguided leaders.”

Narrated by Ibn Abī ʿĀṣim in as-Sunnah.

What occurs of revolutions, rebellion against the ruler, and igniting the fires of tribulation is due to ignorance of the correct aqīdah—the aqīdah of the righteous predecessors (as-salaf aṣ-ṣāliḥ).

**

Learning the aqīdah from its pure source is a fortress against tribulations, a source of safety and security, and a great happiness in this world and the next.**

**And this is something that is incumbent upon us:** to learn the aqīdah—the aqīdah of the prophets and messengers—so that we may attain Paradise and tread the path of salvation at a time when tribulations are like pieces of a dark night.
