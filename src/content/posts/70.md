بسم الله الرحمـٰن الرحيم

يقول الشاعر:

اذا غامرتَ في شرفٍ مرومِ

فلا تقنعْ بما دونَ النجومِ

فطعمُ الموتِ في أمرٍ صغيرٍ

كطعمِ الموتِ في أمرٍ عظيمِ

"If you dare to pursue a lofty honor,

Then do not be content with less than the stars.

For the taste of death for the sake of a small matter

Is like the taste of death for the sake of a great matter."

whatever you set out to seek, whatever you make your goal, then strive for it fully. Aim high. For you will endure hardship regardless.

Every path in life contains difficulty. No one escapes struggle. But there are different kinds of struggle, and not all hardships are equal.

When you choose a path of disobedience to Allah ﷻ, that itself is a hardship. It burdens the heart, darkens the soul, and leads to regret — even if it appears easy in the moment.

And when you choose to obey Allah, to hold firmly to the Qur’an and the Sunnah, that too is a hardship. It requires patience, restraint, and steadfastness.

But the difference is that one hardship leads to loss, And the other leads to salvation.

And surely people will not make the latter easy for you.

They will call you extreme. They will say you are too harsh on yourself. They will tell you to “relax,” to “be moderate,” [despite not understanding what moderation even is] to “stop making the religion difficult.”

And facing this pressure is not easy — especially when it comes from those closest to you.

Allah ﷻ told us in the Qurʾān:

أَحَسِبَ النَّاسُ أَنْ يُتْرَكُوا أَنْ يَقُولُوا آمَنَّا وَهُمْ لَا يُفْتَنُونَ ○ وَلَقَدْ فَتَنَّا الَّذِينَ مِنْ قَبْلِهِمْ ۖ فَلَيَعْلَمَنَّ اللَّهُ الَّذِينَ صَدَقُوا وَلَيَعْلَمَنَّ الْكَاذِبِينَ

“Do people think that they will be left to say, ‘We believe,’ and they will not be tested? And We certainly tested those before them, so Allah will surely make evident those who are truthful, and He will surely make evident the liars." [Al-ʿAnkabout: 2-3]

These tests are a separation between those who merely claim īmān and those who are truthful in it.

So be among the truthful. Remain steadfast.

If hardship is inevitable, then choose the hardship that brings you closer to Allah.

Choose the hardship that ends in Jannah.

Al-Ḥajjāj رحمه الله said:

“Indeed, being patient in refraining from what Allah has forbidden is easier than being patient with His punishment.”

[ʿUddat aṣ-Ṣābirīn wa Dhakhīrat ash-Shākirīn, p. 14]

Allah ﷻ says:

وَمَن يَتَّقِ ٱللَّهَ يَجْعَل لَّهُۥ مَخْرَجًۭا ۝ وَيَرْزُقْهُ مِنْ حَيْثُ لَا يَحْتَسِبُ

“And whoever fears Allah — He will make for him a way out, and provide for him from where he does not expect.” [Al-Ṭalāq: 2-3]

Ask Allah for steadfastness, and He will grant it.

No servant sincerely asks Allah for firmness upon the truth, for beneficial knowledge, or for any form of khayr — except that Allah answers.

To believe otherwise is سوء ظن بالله. (having bad thoughts of Allah ﷻ)

So walk forward, upon the truth, even if your steps are small.

Hold firm, even if you stand alone.

And trust that every hardship you endure for Allah is never lost.

والله أعلم.
