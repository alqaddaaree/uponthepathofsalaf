I (Ibn al-Jawzī) said: I know that the annulment of the treaty regarding women refers to the revelation of the test concerning their rights. So the Messenger of Allāh ﷺ tested her, and tested the women after her. He would say to them: "By Allāh, nothing caused you to leave except love for Allāh, His Messenger, and ʾIslām. You did not leave for the sake of a husband or wealth." When they said that, he would keep them and not send them back to their families.

Umm Kulthūm was a young unmarried woman at that time, so Zayd ibn Ḥārithah married her. After he was killed, she married Az-Zubayr, and she bore him Zaynab. Then she married ʿAbdurRaḥmān ibn ʿAwf, and she bore him Ibrāhīm and Ḥumayd. Then she married ʿAmr ibn al-ʿĀṣ, and she died in his care, may Allāh have mercy on her.

(2/2)

> Ṣifat aṣ-Ṣafwah by Ibn al-Jawzī (1/322–323)

Related: Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī (2711)
