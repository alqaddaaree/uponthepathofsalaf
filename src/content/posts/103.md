بسم الله الرحمن الرحيم

Do you need motivation to pray Qiyām al-Layl?

What can be a better motivator than seeing how your Rabb describes the righteous — those He loves, those He praises in the Qurʾān, those He raises in rank?

Do you not want to be from the people about whom Allāh ﷻ says:

﴿تَتَجَافَىٰ جُنُوبُهُمۡ عَنِ ٱلۡمَضَاجِعِ یَدۡعُونَ رَبَّهُمۡ خَوۡفࣰا وَطَمَعࣰا وَمِمَّا رَزَقۡنَـٰهُمۡ یُنفِقُونَ﴾

Their sides forsake their beds, to invoke their Lord in fear and hope, and they spend (in charity in Allâh’s Cause) out of what We have bestowed on them.

[As-Sajdah, Ayah 16]

﴿إِنَّ ٱلۡمُتَّقِینَ فِی جَنَّـٰتࣲ وَعُیُونٍ  ٠  ءَاخِذِینَ مَاۤ ءَاتَىٰهُمۡ رَبُّهُمۡۚ إِنَّهُمۡ كَانُوا۟ قَبۡلَ ذَ ٰ⁠لِكَ مُحۡسِنِینَ ٠ كَانُوا۟ قَلِیلࣰا مِّنَ ٱلَّیۡلِ مَا یَهۡجَعُونَ ٠ وَبِٱلۡأَسۡحَارِ هُمۡ یَسۡتَغۡفِرُونَ﴾

Verily, the Muttaqûn (the pious) will be in the midst of Gardens and Springs (in the Paradise), Taking joy in the things which their Lord has given them. Verily, they were before this Muhsinûn (good-doers). They used to sleep but little by night [invoking their Lord and praying, with fear and hope]. And in the hours before dawn, they were (found) asking (Allāh) for forgiveness.

[Adh-Dhāriyāt, 15-18]

…or would you like to be from those who turn over in their warm beds while their Rabb descends to the lowest heaven saying: “*Who is calling upon Me that I may answer him? Who is asking from Me that I may give him? Who is seeking My forgiveness that I may forgive him?*” [S‌ah‌i‌h‌ al-Bukha‌ri‌ 1145]

Your alarm rings.

Your bed feels comfortable.

Shayṭān whispers, “Just five more minutes.”

But the pious whisper, “**For Allāh**.”

This life is short.

Comfort is temporary.

Jannah is forever.

Every night you are given a choice:

the comfort of your bed — or the honour of standing before your Rabb, seeking closeness to Him ﷻ.

The question isn’t whether you can wake up.

It’s whether Allāh is worth more than sleep.

And He is always worth more.

Your Rabb is calling you while others are dreaming.

So which will you choose?

> عَنْ سَهْلِ بْنِ سَعْدٍ قَالَ جَاءَ جِبْرِيلُ إِلَى النَّبِيِّ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وَسَلَّمَ فَقَالَ يَا مُحَمَّدُ عِشْ مَا شِئْتَ فَإِنَّكَ مَيِّتٌ وَاعْمَلْ مَا شِئْتَ فَإِنَّكَ مَجْزِيٌّ بِهِ وَأَحْبِبْ مَنْ شِئْتَ فَإِنَّكَ مُفَارِقُهُ وَاعْلَمْ أَنَّ شَرَفَ الْمُؤْمِنِ قِيَامُ اللَّيْلِ وَعِزَّهُ اسْتِغْنَاؤُهُ عَنِ النَّاسِ

Sahl ibn Sa’d reported: Jibrīl came to the Prophet ﷺ and he said, “O Muhammad, live as you wish, for you will surely die. Work as you wish, for you will surely be rewarded according to it. Love whomever you wish, for you will surely depart from them. Know that the nobility of the believer is in prayer at night, and his honor is in his independence of people.”

[al-Muʻjam al-Awsat‌ lil-T‌abara‌ni‌ 4278; Hasan according to Al-Albānī]
