قال ابن القيم في طريق الهجرتين (ص٦٣):

**فالسعيد الرابح من عامل الله فيهم ولم يعاملهم في الله ، وخاف الله فيهم ولم يخفهم في الله وأرضى الله بسخطهم ولم يرضهم بسخط الله، وراقب الله فيهم ولم يراقبهم في الله، وأثر الله عليهم ولم يؤثرهم فى الله، وأمات خوفهم ورجاءهم وحبهم من قلبه وأحى حب الله وخوفه ورجاءَه فيه، فهذا هو الذي يكتب عليهم، وتكون معاملته لهم كلها ربحاً، بشرط أن يصبر على أذاهم ويتخذه مغنماً لا مغرماً وربحاً لا خسراناً.**

> Ibn al-Qayyim said in Ṭarīq al-Hijratayn (p. 63):

The truly happy and successful one is he who deals with Allah concerning the people, and does not deal with the people concerning Allah; who fears Allah with regard to them, and does not fear them with regard to Allah; who pleases Allah even if it angers them, and does not please them at the cost of Allah’s displeasure; who observes Allah concerning them, and does not observe them concerning Allah; who gives precedence to Allah over them, and does not give them precedence over Allah. He causes fear of them, hope in them, and love of them to die in his heart, and gives life to it by love of Allah, fear of Him, and hope in Him. This is the one upon whom they are written (i.e., decreed, meaning: Allah grants him acceptance, influence, and decree over their hearts), and whose dealings with them are entirely profitable—on the condition that he is patient with their harm and takes it as a gain, not a loss, and as profit, not defeat.
